Grievance Redressal Policy

The Digital Estate of Bhargavsinh Sisodiya is strictly committed to maintaining the highest standards of digital integrity, historical accuracy, copyright compliance under the Copyright Act, 1957, data privacy under the DPDP Act, 2023, and overall ethical governance under the Information Technology (IT) Act, 2000. If any user, individual, or authorized entity identifies legitimate concerns regarding published content, ownership rights, data handling, or operational matters, they are formally invited to submit a structured grievance for review.

10.1 Detailed Submission Prerequisites

To prevent frivolous claims and ensure prompt, objective verification, all grievances must be submitted in writing via electronic mail. Anonymous, vague, or incomplete submissions will be summarily rejected without acknowledgment. A valid grievance notice must explicitly contain:

  • Specific Identification: The exact URL, precise page name, or exact section where the alleged issue resides.
  • Comprehensive Nature of Issue: A clear, detailed description explaining why the content constitutes an infringement, factual error, or legal violation.
  • Verifiable Legal or Factual Basis: Substantial evidentiary proof, authentic historical documentation, or statutory proof of ownership supporting the claim.
  • Complainant Credentials: Full legal name, official contact number, physical residential/business address, and a valid corporate or personal email address.
  • Good Faith Legal Declaration: A legally binding signed or electronic statement affirming that the information provided in the complaint is entirely accurate, truthful, and submitted in absolute good faith under penalty of law.

10.2 Comprehensive Review Mechanism & Timelines

Upon receipt of a fully compliant written grievance notice, a structured investigation protocol is initiated:

  • Initial Acknowledgment: Standard acknowledgment of receipt will be issued via email within 24 to 48 business hours.
  • Objective Investigation: The grievance officer will cross-reference internal records, historical reference texts, code logs, or legal precedents to assess authenticity.
  • Statutory Disposal Window: A substantive formal response, corrective action, or reasoned conclusion will be delivered within 15 to 30 working days, subject to the legal complexity of the matter.

10.3 Resolution Approach & Remedial Actions

Following the conclusion of the review process, the Author or designated Grievance Officer maintains the sole administrative discretion to enact appropriate remedial measures, including:

  • Contextual Clarification: Providing official historical context, academic references, or documentary clarifications to the complainant.
  • Correction, Modification, or Takedown: Executing textual amendments, updating archives, or permanently removing disputed content if the grievance is proven to be legally justified and substantiated.
  • Formal Dismissal: Rejecting the grievance with an explanatory note if the complaint lacks evidentiary merit, legal standing, or factual accuracy.

10.5 Strict Misuse, False Claims & Defamation Liability

This grievance mechanism is instituted exclusively for handling genuine, legally grounded concerns. Any frivolous, abusive, repetitive, malicious, or fabricated complaints intended to harass, censor, defame, or disrupt the Digital Estate will be outright dismissed.

Furthermore, any person who knowingly submits a materially false statement or bad-faith takedown claim shall be held legally liable for all resultant damages, financial losses, legal costs, and attorney's fees incurred by the Author under the applicable provisions of the Information Technology Act and Penal laws of India.

10.6 Exhaustion of Internal Dispute Resolution

Users and entities agree that before initiating any external legal proceedings, regulatory complaints, or formal litigation, they shall first exhaust this internal grievance redressal mechanism by submitting a complete notice and allowing the Author the full stipulated review window to investigate and resolve the matter amicably in good faith.

10.7 Governing Law & Legal Jurisdiction

This Grievance Policy and all associated actions or disputes arising from it shall be interpreted, governed, and enforced exclusively in accordance with the laws of India. Any legal action or formal proceeding must be filed and adjudicated exclusively before the competent courts situated within the state of Gujarat, India.

10.8 Modifications & Finality

The Author retains the absolute prerogative to modify, amend, or update this Grievance Redressal Policy at any given time without prior individual notification. Continued access or interaction with this portal following any alterations constitutes formal acceptance of the revised policy guidelines.
